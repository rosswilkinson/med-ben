Claim job expenses

You may be able to claim tax relief if you have to use your own money for things that you must buy or maintain for your job.

You can either claim:

  • for what you’ve spent - you’ll need to keep receipts
  • a ‘flat rate deduction’

This service lets you claim a 'flat rate deduction' for expenses in the tax year from 6 April 2015 to 5 April 2016.

What are Flat Rate Deductions?

Flat rate deductions are set amounts that HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) has agreed are typically spent each year by employees in different occupations.


You must verify your identity to use this service for the first time. You’ll need:

  • a valid UK passport or UK photocard driving licence
  • access to your financial information

You only have to verify your identity once. After that you can sign in with your username and password.

Before you start

You can use this service if you:

  • have only one job
  • did that job for all or some of the tax year from 6 April 2015 to 5 April 2016
  • are claiming no more than £165

You can’t claim flat rate expenses if your employer:

  • pays the expenses for you
  • provides vouchers or some other way of covering the cost
  • deducts the payment from your pay, before deducting income tax (this is called a ‘net pay arrangement’)

This service is still being developed. It doesn't yet include every occupation. If you’re not able to use it you may be able to claim using form P87
