HM Revenue and Customs

 

Check or update your medical insurance company benefit

Tell HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) about changes to your medical insurance benefit that happen between 6 April 2015 and 5 April 2016.

Use this service to:

  • check if you’re currently paying tax on a medical insurance benefit
  • tell HMRC that you’ve stopped receiving a medical insurance benefit
  • tell HMRC to remove a medical insurance benefit that you don’t get

You must verify your identity to use this service for the first time. You’ll need:

  • a valid UK passport or UK photocard driving licence
  • access to your financial information

You only have to verify your identity once, after that you can sign in with your username and password.

Before you start

If you ended your medical insurance benefit on or after 6 April 2015, you’ll need to ask your employer how much they paid for it from 6 April 2015 until the date it ended.

Your employer tells HMRC what company benefits they’ve given you at the end of each tax year in form P11D. This form is used to make sure that the amount of tax you pay correct.

You can’t use this service to:

  • add a new medical insurance benefit
  • change the amount of insurance that you still get from an employer
  • update the details of benefits you had before 6 April 2015
